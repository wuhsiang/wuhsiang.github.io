As a student graduated from Business School, I strongly admire ‘the second vision‘ explained by James March: this tradition sees action as based not on anticipations of consequences but on attempts to fulfill the obligations of personal and social identities and senses of self.

Quixote reminds us that if we trust only when trust is warranted, love only when love is returned, learn only when learning is valuable, we abandon an essential feature of our humanness — our willingness to act in the name of a conception of ourselves regardless of its consequences.
By James March, A Scholar’s Quest

The most valuable lesson I have learnt in the past years is that I strongly believe the choice

Two roads diverged in a wood, and I —
I took the one less traveled by,
And that has made all the difference.
Robert Frost, The Road Not Taken

But I had not realized that I have to work twice as hard to break through the clutter for the fabulous scenery.

作為商學院的學生，我深深地認同James March所說的“第二個視角”：這種傳統認為，人的行為不是基於預期結果，而是為了履行責任、實現自我。

堂吉訶德提醒我們，如果我們只在不被辜負的時候去信任，只在有所回報的時候去愛，只在學有所用的時候去學習，那麼我們就放棄了人的本質特征—願意在自我概念的名義下行動，而不論結果如何。
James March, 一個學者的追求

這些年來，最深刻的教訓是：我深深地相信如下的選擇：

森林里分出了兩條岔路，
而我卻選擇了人跡更少的一條，
從此決定了我的歸宿

然而，我在過去卻未能意識到，為此我需要比別人付出雙倍的努力，去披荊斬棘，方能欣賞絕世的風景。
